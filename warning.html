Warning and Prevention


They do much more than just decorate our messages—they inject nuance, humor, and vital emotional context into otherwise flat text.


Emojis look innocent, but many of them carry double entendres or hidden slang meanings within youth culture. The Risk: Certain combinations of food emojis (like the eggplant, peach, or banana) or symbols (like the water droplets) are frequently used to bypass word filters to communicate mature themes. Many predators and youth use "coded" emojis to bypass text.


Review messaging behaviors for patterns rather than isolated symbols. Use age-appropriate parental control and monitoring applications to alert you if your child is receiving unfamiliar or concerning emoji combinations. Talk openly with kids about hidden meanings, and establish rules like the "Screenshot and Save" rule to document harassment before blocking. Pay attention to your child's behavior. You know your child best, there will be red flags. PAY ATTENTION to them.
